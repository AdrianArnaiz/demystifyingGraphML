
# Oversmoothing, "Oversquashing", Heterophily, Long-Range, and more: Demystifying Common Beliefs in Graph Machine Learning
* Authors: ***Adrian Arnaiz-Rodriguez** and **Federico Errica*** 
* Accepted at: **The Fourteenth International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR 2026)**
* 📄 Available at [OpenReview](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Q3MisVkuTu) and [arXiv](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Q3MisVkuTu).
---

## Summary

This position paper critically examines widely held assumptions in graph machine learning, particularly around:

* Oversmoothing
* Oversquashing
* Homophily vs. heterophily
* Long-range propagation

We argue that rapid progress in message-passing GNN research has produced **conflated or overstated beliefs**, creating ambiguity in how these phenomena are defined and studied. The paper provides conceptual clarification and counterexamples to disentangle these challenges and encourage more precise research directions.
